## The Nigerian Business Hierarchy Problem

One of the biggest sales challenges in Nigeria is getting to the right person. Companies often have formal title structures that don't reflect actual decision-making authority. The "Head of Procurement" might be executing decisions made by the CFO or MD. The IT Manager might influence the vendor selection but not approve the budget.

Identifying who actually decides — and getting a direct contact — is the foundational work of B2B prospecting.

## Where Decision-Makers Are Findable

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn remains the most reliable source for Nigerian corporate contacts, particularly in Lagos and Abuja. Key filters to use:

- **Company name** + **seniority level** (Director, VP, C-Suite)
- **Function** (Finance, Operations, Technology, Procurement)
- **Industry** to narrow to your target sector

Connection requests with a relevant note convert better than InMail for Nigerian professionals. A second-degree connection from someone they know can double your response rate.

### Company Websites

Many Nigerian companies list leadership teams, department heads, or "Contact us" pages with named individuals. Check:

- The **About Us** and **Team** pages
- **Press releases** — these often name executives with titles
- **Annual reports** (for listed companies like Dangote Cement, GTBank, MTN Nigeria)

### Email Pattern Matching

Once you know a name and the company's email domain, you can often infer the email format:

- `firstname.lastname@company.com.ng`
- `f.lastname@company.com.ng`
- `firstname@company.com.ng`

Email verification tools confirm which format is active before you send.

### Industry Events and Associations

Nigeria has active industry associations — LCCI, MAN, NACCIMA — whose member directories and event attendee lists are valuable. A decision-maker who spoke at a conference or signed an industry statement is both identifiable and warm.

## Titles That Usually Signal Budget Authority

For most B2B products in Nigeria, target these roles:

| Product/Service | Decision-Maker Titles |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Software/SaaS | CTO, IT Director, Head of Digital |
| Marketing services | CMO, Head of Brand, Marketing Director |
| Logistics/supply chain | COO, Head of Supply Chain, Operations Director |
| Financial services | CFO, Finance Director, Treasurer |
| HR/payroll | Head of HR, People Director |

Managing Directors and CEOs are decision-makers at SMBs but often not the right starting point at larger enterprises — they'll delegate the evaluation anyway.

## Warm Vs Cold Contact

A cold email to a named decision-maker with a relevant subject line outperforms a call to a switchboard by a significant margin. But a warm referral — someone who knows both you and the prospect — outperforms both.

When enriching your contact list:
1. Check whether any of your existing customers know the target
2. Engage with the prospect on LinkedIn before outreaching
3. Reference a specific business event (new funding, expansion news, a regulatory change in their sector)

The goal is not to find any contact — it's to find the right contact and reach them with a reason they care about.
