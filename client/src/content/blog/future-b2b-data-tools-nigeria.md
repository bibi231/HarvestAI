The global B2B data industry is worth over $3 billion annually. Nigeria's share of that market is tiny — but it's growing fast. Here's the trajectory.

## Where Nigeria's B2B data market is today

Most Nigerian B2B teams still rely on:
- Manual Google research
- Personal networks and referrals
- Purchased lists from local data brokers (often outdated)
- LinkedIn manual prospecting

This isn't because Nigerian businesses are behind — it's because the tooling hasn't existed at sufficient quality and local relevance. International tools like ZoomInfo and Apollo have limited Nigerian data coverage. Local alternatives have been slow to emerge.

HarvestAI fills this gap: web extraction, lead generation, email finding, and enrichment calibrated for Nigerian sources.

## The four forces accelerating adoption

**1. SaaS adoption surge:** More Nigerian companies are buying software. Software companies need B2B data to prospect efficiently. Demand pulls the infrastructure.

**2. Formalization pressure:** CAC registration, FIRS compliance, and digital payment infrastructure are creating data trails. Businesses that didn't appear in any database 5 years ago are now findable.

**3. AI cost reduction:** The cost of intelligent web extraction has dropped dramatically with LLMs. What required expensive custom scrapers in 2022 can be done with an API call in 2026.

**4. Competitive pressure:** As more Nigerian B2B companies adopt data tools, businesses that don't will lose ground. Early adoption advantage is compressing.

## What's coming in 12–24 months

### Real-time intent data

In Western markets, intent data (signals that a company is actively researching a category — visiting competitor sites, searching specific terms) commands premium pricing. For Nigeria, intent data is nascent but building.

Early signals already trackable: job postings for specific roles, recent website updates, social media activity patterns, Google Maps review velocity.

### Deeper CRM integration

Current tools produce CSVs that require manual import. The next step: direct integration with Nigerian-friendly CRMs, so leads flow into Pipedrive, HubSpot, or custom systems automatically.

HarvestAI's API enables this for developers today. No-code connectors via Zapier and Make are coming.

### Voice and WhatsApp outreach data

Nigerian B2B is WhatsApp-first in many sectors. Phone numbers plus WhatsApp status signals will become standard enrichment fields. Business WhatsApp API integration enables structured outreach to verified WhatsApp numbers.

### Cross-border African data

Nigerian companies increasingly sell across West Africa (Ghana, Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire) and beyond. B2B data that covers the broader Anglophone and Francophone African market — not just Nigeria — is the logical extension.

## The competitive landscape forming

Tools competing in the Nigerian B2B data space:
- **Global platforms (Apollo, ZoomInfo):** Growing Nigeria coverage but still sparse outside Lagos/Abuja tech companies
- **Regional African platforms:** Expanding from South Africa and East Africa
- **HarvestAI:** Built specifically for Nigerian and West African data sources from day one

The Nigerian-built advantage: understanding of local data sources (VConnect, CAC, state business registries), Nigerian address formats, local phone number formats, and the specific industries that dominate Nigerian B2B.
