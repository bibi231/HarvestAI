## The Question Every Nigerian Sales Team Asks

When you need to find businesses in Lagos, Abuja, or Port Harcourt, two sources come up constantly: VConnect and Google Maps. Both are free to browse. Both list Nigerian businesses. But they serve different purposes and have different strengths.

## VConnect: Built for Nigerian Business Discovery

VConnect was designed specifically as a Nigerian business directory. Its advantages:

- **Category taxonomy** mapped to Nigerian market structures — it distinguishes between a clearing agent and a freight forwarder, which Google often doesn't
- **Direct contact fields**: phone numbers, email addresses, and often a physical address
- **Industry filtering** that reflects actual Nigerian sectors (agro-processing, building materials, etc.)
- Coverage of **formal SMBs** that are registered but too small to rank on Google

The weaknesses are real though. VConnect data ages badly. Many listings have phone numbers that go to old SIM cards, or emails that bounce. Update frequency is inconsistent — a company that closed in 2023 may still appear active.

## Google Maps: Broader, But Noisier

Google Maps wins on volume. Lagos alone has hundreds of thousands of business pins. Key strengths:

- **User-verified updates** — customers leave reviews and flag closed businesses, so data degrades more slowly
- **Photos and operating hours** give context that a directory listing cannot
- **Geo-precision**: you can pull every business within 2km of Trade Fair Complex or along Adetokunbo Ademola Street
- Good at surfacing **micro-businesses** — salons, pharmacies, workshops — that would never self-list on VConnect

The gap is contact depth. Google Maps rarely gives you a named contact person or a direct email. You get a business phone number and sometimes a website. If you need the MD's direct line or the procurement manager's email, Google Maps gets you only partway there.

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Dimension | VConnect | Google Maps |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Geographic coverage | Good in major cities | Excellent nationwide |
| Named contacts | Sometimes | Rarely |
| Email data | Often included | Not included |
| Data freshness | Moderate | Better (crowd-updated) |
| Industry filtering | Strong | Basic |
| Rural/peri-urban coverage | Weak | Better |

## The Right Approach: Use Both Together

For **B2B prospecting in formal sectors** (manufacturing, distribution, professional services), start with VConnect to get a structured list with industry codes and contact fields, then cross-check with Google Maps to verify the business is still operating and to pick up any additional contact details from their website link.

For **geographic territory coverage** — mapping every competitor, distributor, or retailer in a region — start with Google Maps for volume, then enrich the records with VConnect and LinkedIn lookups.

Neither source alone gives you a complete picture. The companies that win at Nigerian lead generation treat both as raw inputs, not finished databases.
