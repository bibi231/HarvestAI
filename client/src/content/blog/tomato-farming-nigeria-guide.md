Web scraping sounds technical. It isn't hard to understand, and its business applications are straightforward. Here's the plain-language version.

## What web scraping is

When you visit a website, your browser reads the page's code and displays it visually. Web scraping does the same thing — but instead of displaying the page, it extracts specific information and saves it as structured data.

Example: You want a list of all hotels in Abuja with their phone numbers, prices, and websites. Manually, you'd Google each one, click through, copy the details, and paste them into a spreadsheet. That takes hours.

A web scraper visits those pages automatically, pulls the relevant fields (name, phone, website, price range), and returns a spreadsheet in minutes.

## What web scraping is not

It's not hacking. It's not accessing private information. It's not stealing.

Web scraping reads publicly visible information — the same information you could read yourself by visiting the page. The only difference is speed and scale.

Where it crosses lines: scraping behind logins without permission, scraping at volumes that harm the target server, or using scraped data for purposes the site explicitly prohibits in its terms of service.

For business intelligence purposes — competitive research, lead generation, market data — scraping public pages is widely accepted and legally defensible in most jurisdictions.

## The five main uses for Nigerian businesses

### 1. Lead generation

Find potential customers by scraping Nigerian business directories, Google Maps, and industry listings. Output: a list of businesses that match your target profile, with contact information.

### 2. Competitive price monitoring

Track what competitors charge on Jumia, Konga, or their own websites. Automatically updated, so you always know if the market moved without you.

### 3. Market research

Understand a market by scraping data at scale. How many competitors are there in a given category in Lagos? What price ranges do they operate in? What keywords do they use?

### 4. Job postings intelligence

Scrape competitor job listings to understand their hiring strategy, technology stack (from job requirements), and growth direction.

### 5. Data collection for analysis

Any publicly visible data that would help you make decisions — customer reviews on competitor products, property prices in different Lagos neighborhoods, scholarship listings for a directory site.
