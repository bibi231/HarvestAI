B2B lead generation in Nigeria has always been relationship-driven. You get introduced, attend events, ask for referrals. That works — but it scales slowly. AI-powered lead generation adds a third channel: finding qualified prospects at scale using public data.

## What B2B lead generation tools actually do

Modern lead generation tools scrape public sources — business directories, LinkedIn, company websites, Google Maps, industry registrations — and compile structured data about businesses that match your target criteria.

For a Nigerian company selling SaaS to Lagos SMEs, that might mean:
- All registered businesses in Lagos in a specific industry
- Companies with websites that show specific technology signals
- Businesses that have recently posted job listings (sign of growth)
- Companies in a specific revenue bracket based on office location and staff count signals

## The quality problem with Nigerian lead lists

Bought lead lists in Nigeria are notoriously low quality. Common problems:
- Outdated contacts (people leave companies, numbers change)
- Wrong industry categorization
- Missing emails (replaced with generic info@)
- Duplicate entries
- Businesses that have closed

The advantage of scraping fresh data: it reflects current reality. A business that shows active Google Reviews and a recently updated website is more likely to be operational than one in a 3-year-old database.

## What makes a quality Nigerian B2B lead

A lead worth pursuing has:
1. **Verified contact** — email or phone that actually works
2. **Active business signals** — website updated in last 12 months, social media active, Google Maps listing shows recent reviews
3. **Fit signals** — company size, industry, and location match your ideal customer profile
4. **Decision-maker access** — you can reach someone with buying authority, not just an info@ address

HarvestAI's relevance score rates each lead on these factors so you can prioritize your outreach.

## Building your ideal customer profile first

Before generating leads, define who you're looking for:

**Industry:** Which business categories? (Manufacturing, hospitality, professional services, education, healthcare, retail)

**Geography:** Lagos Island only? All of Lagos? Specific states?

**Size:** Are you targeting micro businesses (1–5 employees), SMEs (10–100), or enterprise?

**Tech signals:** Do they need to have a website? A social media presence? A specific technology installed?

**Decision maker:** Who buys your product — the founder, the IT manager, the marketing manager?

The more specific your ICP, the more useful your lead list. 100 highly-targeted leads will outperform 2,000 generic ones in conversion.

## The right way to use scraped leads

1. **Verify before outreach** — run email addresses through a verifier (HarvestAI's Enrich mode does this)
2. **Personalize, don't blast** — a mention of something specific to their business converts 3–5x better than generic pitches
3. **Layer channels** — email + LinkedIn connection + WhatsApp where appropriate
4. **Track and iterate** — which source generates the best response rate? Double down on it
