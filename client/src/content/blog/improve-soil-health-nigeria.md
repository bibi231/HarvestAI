Web scraping — automatically extracting data from websites — powers some of the most valuable business intelligence tools in the world. For Nigerian companies, it's an underutilized competitive advantage.

## What web scraping is (and isn't)

Web scraping automates the process of reading public web pages and extracting structured data from them. Instead of manually copying information from 500 company pages into a spreadsheet, a scraper does it in minutes.

**What you can typically scrape legally:**
- Public business listings (Google Maps, directory sites)
- Publicly visible prices on e-commerce sites
- Publicly visible contact information (names, emails on contact pages)
- SERP (search engine results pages) for ranking and keyword research
- Company information from business registries

**What creates legal and ethical problems:**
- Scraping data behind a login without permission
- Bypassing CAPTCHAs or access controls
- Scraping at volume that constitutes a denial of service
- Scraping and republishing copyrighted content
- Using scraped data in ways that violate NDPR (Nigeria Data Protection Regulation)

The key principle: if data is publicly visible without authentication, scraping it is generally acceptable. Using the scraped data in harmful ways is where legal risk emerges.

## Practical use cases for Nigerian businesses

### Competitive price monitoring

An e-commerce business wants to know if competitors adjust prices on Jumia, Konga, or their own websites. Automated price monitoring tracks competitor prices and alerts you when they change.

Result: you adjust pricing strategies based on market data rather than guesswork.

### Lead generation from directories

VConnect, BusinessList Nigeria, Nairaland business listings, and Google Maps list thousands of Nigerian businesses with contact information. Scraping these gives you a structured prospect database faster than manual research.

### Job listings intelligence

Monitoring competitor job postings reveals hiring trends, technology stack clues (a DevOps hire suggests infrastructure scaling), and business direction signals.

### Market research at scale

Understanding how a market is structured — which players exist, how they price, what their website messaging is — typically requires hours of manual research. Scraping compresses it to minutes.

### SERP tracking

Monitoring your rankings and competitors' rankings for target keywords in Nigerian Google search results helps prioritize SEO investment.

## NDPR considerations for Nigerian businesses

Nigeria's Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) governs how personal data is collected, stored, and used. Key points for scraped data:
- Email addresses are personal data under NDPR
- You must have a lawful basis for using them (legitimate interest is a common basis for B2B outreach)
- You must provide a way for people to opt out
- Data must not be sold or shared without consent

For B2B lead generation using publicly listed business contacts (not personal consumer data), the risk profile is low but not zero. Maintain good outreach hygiene: include unsubscribe options, don't spam, don't share lists.
