In a fast-moving market like Nigeria, what your competitor charges, offers, or says today might change tomorrow. Manual monitoring doesn't scale. Here's how to automate it.

## What's worth monitoring on competitor websites

**Pricing pages:** Most Nigerian SaaS products and services publish pricing publicly. Any change is immediately relevant to your positioning.

**Product/service pages:** New features, new service tiers, new offerings. Signals investment direction and areas they're competing harder.

**Job postings:** Tells you which teams they're growing. Hiring 3 engineers → building something. Hiring 2 sales reps → growing commercial motion.

**Blog/content:** What are they teaching their market? What keywords are they targeting? What use cases are they building authority in?

**Customer testimonials and case studies:** Who are their customers? What results are they claiming? Identifies the market segments they're winning.

**Promotions and offers:** Temporary discounts, free trials, limited-time offers. A competitor running an aggressive promotion is usually a signal.

## Common competitor monitoring workflows

### Pricing watch

Weekly extract of competitor pricing pages → spreadsheet → formula that highlights any cell that changed. Takes 2 minutes to review each week.

### Hire signal tracking

Monthly scrape of competitor job postings page → compare to last month → new roles appear in diff. Analyze: what does hiring a "Head of Partnerships" mean for their strategy?

### Content gap tracking

Monthly SERP run for 100 target keywords → filter for keywords where competitor ranks top 5 but you don't → these are content opportunities they're building authority in.

### Feature release monitoring

Quarterly extract of competitor product/feature pages → compare to prior version → new sections or features appear in diff.

## Making sense of the data

Raw monitoring data needs interpretation:
- A price drop: aggressive acquisition move, or testing price sensitivity?
- A new feature announcement: response to customer demand, or trying to expand market?
- Rapid hiring in customer success: churning customers, or growing fast?

Context matters. Combine monitoring data with:
- Win/loss conversation notes from your sales team
- Customer feedback about competitors
- Industry news and PR

## The Nigerian competitive intelligence advantage

Many Nigerian businesses don't do systematic competitor monitoring. This means:
- Being surprised by competitor promotions that eat into your pipeline
- Missing competitive weaknesses you could exploit
- Losing deals to claims you weren't aware your competitor was making

A basic monitoring setup (6–8 pages, monthly runs) catches 80% of meaningful changes.
