## The FMCG Pricing Problem in Nigeria

Nigeria's FMCG market is intensely competitive. Danone, Nestlé, Unilever, and hundreds of local manufacturers fight for shelf space across modern trade (Shoprite, Spar, Ebeano) and the vast informal trade network of open markets and neighbourhood kiosks.

The challenge: pricing is not uniform. A 500ml bottle of a popular tomato paste may sell for ₦850 at a Lagos supermarket and ₦780 from a distributor in Onitsha. When your competitor adjusts distributor prices or runs a trade promotion, you often find out weeks after the market has already shifted.

**Price monitoring solves the lag problem** — letting brands track competitor prices in near real-time.

## What FMCG Companies Actually Monitor

Price monitoring in the Nigerian context covers several layers:

- **Retail shelf prices** — what end consumers pay at modern trade outlets
- **Distributor prices** — what goes to wholesalers and depot operators
- **Promo pricing** — temporary discounts, bundle offers, "buy 2 get 1" mechanics
- **Online marketplace pricing** — Jumia, Konga, and B2B platforms like TradeDepot list prices that feed into market expectations

Some companies also monitor **out-of-stock events** — if a competitor's product is consistently unavailable, that's an opportunity your sales team should act on immediately.

## How Automated Price Monitoring Works

Manual price checks — sending field agents to markets — is expensive, slow, and gives you a sample, not the picture. Automated scraping provides:

1. **Scheduled collection** from retailer websites, Jumia, and any competitor website that lists prices
2. **Structured output** into a dashboard or spreadsheet with timestamps, SKU-level detail, and price trend history
3. **Alerts** when a tracked price moves by more than a defined threshold (e.g., 5% drop by a competitor)

The data pipeline typically runs daily or several times per week, giving brand managers a current view without field team costs.

## A Practical Example

An Abuja-based distributor for a major beverage brand used weekly competitor price scraping across five competitor SKUs on Jumia and two distributor platforms. Within the first month, they identified that a competitor had quietly reduced their 1-litre carton price by ₦120 — below the threshold for their own salespeople to notice but significant enough to explain a drop in reorder rates from three key customers.

With that intelligence, they were able to respond with a targeted trade discount before losing the accounts.

## What to Track (A Starter List)

- **Product name and SKU**
- **Listed price and any promotional price**
- **Platform or retailer**
- **Date and time of data capture**
- **Availability status (in stock / out of stock)**

Price monitoring is not just a defensive tool. Brands that monitor consistently also identify when competitors pull back on promotions — a signal to push volume while the competitor regroups.
