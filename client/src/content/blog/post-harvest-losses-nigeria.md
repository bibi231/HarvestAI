Price competition in Nigerian e-commerce is intense. Jumia, Konga, and thousands of independent online stores compete on margin daily. Knowing what competitors charge — and reacting fast — requires automation.

## The problem with manual price tracking

A typical Nigerian electronics retailer might compete with 20–30 sellers on Jumia and 10–15 on their own websites. That's potentially 500–600 product-price data points to monitor.

Checking manually:
- Takes 3–5 hours per person per day
- Goes stale immediately (prices change multiple times per day on active listings)
- Doesn't scale as your product catalog grows
- Human error means you miss important changes

Automated monitoring:
- Runs continuously or on a schedule you set
- Alerts you when prices cross a threshold
- Tracks historical trends
- Covers hundreds of products with the same effort as monitoring one

## Common price monitoring strategies

### Match-on-key-SKUs

Don't monitor every product — monitor the 20% that drive 80% of revenue. Keep these priced competitively. Let low-margin, low-volume products price themselves.

### Premium positioning strategy

Some businesses deliberately price 5–10% above market. Monitor ensures you don't accidentally underprice and erode your "premium" signal.

### Floor pricing with dynamic adjustments

Set a minimum margin floor. When competitors drop below a threshold, hold your price and let them take that sale. When competitors raise prices, consider following.

### Promotional detection

Price monitoring detects when a competitor is running a promotion — sudden drops across multiple SKUs. Gives you the option to match, counter, or wait it out.

## Practical setup for a Nigerian retailer

1. Build your competitor list: 5–10 most-watched competitors
2. List key product URLs for each (or product search terms on Jumia/Konga)
3. Set a monitoring schedule (daily for active categories, weekly for stable ones)
4. Define alert thresholds (alert me if any competitor drops more than 10%)
5. Review weekly and adjust pricing accordingly

Time investment: 2 hours setup, 30 minutes per week review.
