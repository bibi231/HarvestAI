## Scraped Data Is Not Sales-Ready by Default

When you pull 2,000 business records from a directory or marketplace, you have raw data — not a pipeline. The gap between a business existing and a business being a viable prospect for your product is wide.

Qualification bridges that gap. Done properly, it means your sales team spends time on leads that can actually close, not chasing records that were never relevant.

## The Four Qualification Dimensions

### 1. Fit

Does this company match your ideal customer profile?

- **Industry match**: Are they in the sector you serve?
- **Size match**: Are they big enough to need your solution and able to afford it?
- **Geography**: Are they in a location you can serve?

A company that fails on fit should be filtered out before it ever reaches a salesperson. No amount of follow-up changes a fundamental mismatch.

### 2. Data Quality

- Is the contact information current and verified?
- Is the company still operating? (Check for recent activity — website last updated, social media posts, Google Maps reviews)
- Is the decision-maker contact still in that role? (LinkedIn "current position" is your first check)

In Nigeria, companies move, rename, and restructure frequently. A directory entry from 18 months ago can be outdated.

### 3. Intent Signals

What evidence suggests this company is currently looking for what you offer?

Look for:

- **Job postings** in relevant functions (a company hiring "Head of CRM" is probably evaluating CRM tools)
- **Funding announcements** — fresh capital often means new vendor evaluations
- **Expansion news** — a new Lagos office suggests growing headcount and procurement activity
- **Industry trigger events** — a regulatory change that forces companies in a sector to upgrade processes

Intent signals separate a cold outreach from a timed one.

### 4. Reachability

Do you have a verified, direct contact for the right person?

A company that fits perfectly but where you have only a general `info@` address is less valuable than a slightly lower-fit company where you have the CFO's verified direct email.

## A Practical Scoring System

Assign points across dimensions:

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| ICP fit (industry + size) | 40 pts |
| Verified direct contact | 25 pts |
| Intent signal present | 25 pts |
| Data freshness (< 6 months) | 10 pts |

**80+**: Priority outreach, personalised approach  
**50–79**: Standard sequence, moderate personalisation  
**Below 50**: Nurture or discard

## Applying This in Practice

Most CRMs (HubSpot, Zoho, Pipedrive) support custom lead scoring fields. After importing scraped data:

1. Run through fit filters to remove irrelevant records
2. Verify contact information (email verification API, phone validator)
3. Run a quick intent check (LinkedIn, news search, job boards)
4. Score and segment
5. Import with scoring into CRM for sequenced outreach

The output should be a smaller, cleaner list — typically 15–30% of the raw data — that your sales team can work with confidence.

Qualification is not about finding reasons to reject leads. It is about making sure every lead your team calls has a real reason to say yes.
