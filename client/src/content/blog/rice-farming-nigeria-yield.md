A referral-only sales pipeline is great until your referral network runs dry. Data-driven prospecting builds a pipeline you can actually forecast and scale.

## The anatomy of a Nigerian B2B sales pipeline

A structured pipeline has defined stages and specific criteria for moving a deal forward:

| Stage | Definition | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Prospect | Identified, not yet contacted | Research and validate |
| Contacted | Outreach sent, no response | Follow-up (2x) |
| Engaged | Responded or took meeting | Qualify and pitch |
| Proposal | Proposal sent | Follow up, handle objections |
| Negotiation | Discussing terms | Close or re-scope |
| Closed Won | Contract signed, payment initiated | Onboard |
| Closed Lost | Deal fell through | Note reason, re-engage in 6 months |

Without this structure, deals stall in undefined states and get forgotten.

## Why data matters at each stage

**Prospect → Contacted:** Better data (right person, verified email, correct phone) means higher contact rates. A list of 200 verified decision-maker emails produces more conversations than 500 unverified generic addresses.

**Contacted → Engaged:** Personalization driven by data (knowing their industry, company size, recent news) increases response rates 3–5x over generic outreach.

**Engaged → Proposal:** Enriched company data (headcount, revenue signals, current technology) helps you scope proposals that match the buyer's context.

**Proposal → Closed:** Timing data (is their fiscal year ending? did they just raise money?) helps prioritize and time follow-ups.

## Building the prospect database

**Source 1: HarvestAI Lead Finder** — Generate lists of target companies by type and location. Each list includes contact data.

**Source 2: Event attendee lists** — Nigerian business events (conferences, expos) often publish attendee or exhibitor lists. These are motivated prospects who invested in attending.

**Source 3: Competitor client signals** — Companies using your competitor's product are proven buyers. LinkedIn case studies, testimonials, and integration directories reveal who's using what.

**Source 4: Job listings** — A company hiring for a role adjacent to your product is signaling relevant needs. (Hiring a "Customer Success Manager" for a company with no CS function → they're scaling their customer base → might need your customer support tool.)

## The Nigerian pipeline math

Working backwards from a revenue target:

- Target: ₦10 million in new revenue per quarter
- Average deal value: ₦500,000
- Deals needed: 20
- Close rate on proposals: 25% → need 80 proposals
- Proposal rate from qualified prospects: 30% → need 267 qualified conversations
- Contact-to-conversation rate: 8% → need 3,340 contacts

This means consistently adding ~250 verified, targeted contacts per week to your outreach pipeline.

Manual research produces 20–30 contacts per day at best. HarvestAI produces 200–500 per run.

## Maintaining pipeline health

Signs of an unhealthy pipeline:
- Most deals are in "Contacted" or "Proposal" with no movement for 30+ days
- You can't accurately forecast next month's revenue
- Pipeline is entirely from referrals (not reproducible at scale)

Signs of a healthy pipeline:
- Steady flow from Prospect → Contacted → Engaged
- Clear data on where deals stall and why
- Mix of referral + outbound + inbound sources
