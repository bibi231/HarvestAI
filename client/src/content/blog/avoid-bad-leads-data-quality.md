A sales team pursuing bad leads isn't just unproductive — it's demoralizing. The cost of bad data in Nigerian B2B sales compounds faster than most businesses realize.

## What makes a lead "bad"

Bad leads fall into four categories:

**1. Invalid:** Wrong email, disconnected phone, company no longer exists. Outreach goes nowhere.

**2. Irrelevant:** Real company, real contact — but wrong fit. Wrong industry, too small, wrong geography.

**3. Unreachable decision-makers:** The contact exists but isn't the buyer. A technical contact at a company where the CFO makes the call, for example.

**4. Already-lost prospects:** Companies that have already committed to a competitor or explicitly rejected your category of product.

A typical unverified scraped list in Nigeria: 25–40% fall into one of these categories.

## The cost of bad leads

**Direct time cost:** If each sales outreach attempt takes 8 minutes (research + compose + send), and 35% of your list is bad, you're spending 28% of your outreach time on zero-return activity.

**Sender reputation cost:** Emails to invalid addresses bounce. High bounce rates (over 5%) cause email providers to route your emails to spam — hurting delivery to your good contacts too.

**Pipeline distortion:** Bad leads in your CRM inflate pipeline numbers and produce unreliable forecasts. You think you have ₦50M in pipeline; you actually have ₦30M.

**Team morale:** Salespeople who spend time on dead leads lose confidence. This affects quality of effort on the real opportunities.

## Data quality checks before outreach

**Step 1: Email verification**
Run all emails through a verifier before the first touch. HarvestAI's Enrich mode does this. Target: remove all emails with < 70% deliverability confidence.

**Step 2: Phone format validation**
Nigerian mobile numbers are 11 digits starting with 070, 080, 081, 090, 091. Numbers not matching this pattern are invalid. Landlines start with 01 (Lagos), 02 (other), etc.

**Step 3: Website check**
Does the company website exist and load? A 404 or parked domain often signals a closed or inactive business.

**Step 4: Activity signals**
Google Maps: any reviews in the last 12 months? Social media: last post within 6 months? Active = likely still operating.

**Step 5: ICP fit scoring**
Score each lead 1–3 on fit: industry match, size match, location match. Prioritize 3/3 leads before touching 2/3 or 1/3.

## Preventing bad data from entering your CRM

**At the point of scraping:** HarvestAI's relevance score filters out low-confidence records. Set a minimum threshold before exporting.

**At the point of import:** Don't import raw scraped lists directly to your CRM. Validate first, then import only verified records.

**Ongoing maintenance:** A quarterly audit — review records that haven't been touched in 6 months, remove clearly stale data.

## The quality vs. quantity tradeoff

A common mistake: maximizing list size and treating quality as a secondary concern.

Better principle: **a list of 200 quality leads produces more revenue than a list of 1,000 poor ones.**
