Procurement teams, startups building a supply chain, and businesses diversifying vendors all face the same problem: finding verified contacts for Nigerian suppliers is slow and unreliable.

## The supplier discovery challenge in Nigeria

Unlike Western markets with comprehensive platforms like ThomasNet or Alibaba with strong Nigerian coverage, supplier discovery in Nigeria relies heavily on:
- Word of mouth and referrals
- Trade associations
- Physical markets and trade fairs
- Google search + manual research

The problem with manual research: it's slow, inconsistent, and produces unstructured results (bookmarks, notes, WhatsApp forwards) rather than a clean database.

## Where Nigerian suppliers list themselves

**Alibaba.com — Nigeria section:** Despite being China-focused, many Nigerian manufacturers and distributors have Alibaba profiles, especially those handling exports or international trade.

**VConnect and BusinessList Nigeria:** Strong for manufacturing, construction materials, and industrial suppliers.

**MAN (Manufacturers Association of Nigeria) directory:** Members include legitimate Nigerian manufacturers. Requires some research but high-quality contacts.

**Lagos Chamber of Commerce directory:** Professional services and trade companies.

**Nigerian Export Promotion Council (NEPC) directory:** Exporters and producers — useful for raw materials and agricultural commodities.

**Jumia Business and Konga wholesale:** Some wholesale suppliers have storefronts here.

**Trade fairs:** LAGOS TRADE FAIR, BuildExpo, Agrofood Nigeria — exhibitor lists are published and highly targetable.

## Validating supplier contacts

Before pursuing a supplier relationship, basic validation:
- **Website check:** Does the site look like an operational business? Recent product updates or news?
- **Registration check:** CAC search (cac.gov.ng) for registered company status
- **Phone verification:** Call the number. Do they answer as the company?
- **Physical address check:** Google Maps — is there a physical presence?
- **References:** Can they provide 2–3 references from current customers?

Data tools get you to the right contacts. Due diligence on the actual supplier relationship remains your own process.

## Building a vendor database

For businesses with recurring procurement needs, maintaining a structured vendor database saves time on every future sourcing decision:

**Columns to maintain:**
- Company name
- Contact name
- Email + phone (verified)
- Specialty / product category
- MOQ (minimum order quantity) if known
- Payment terms (prepay, Net 30, etc.)
- Quality rating (from past experience)
- Last contact date
