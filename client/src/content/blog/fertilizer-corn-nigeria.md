SEO without rank tracking is like driving without a speedometer. You might be moving, but you don't know how fast or in what direction. For Nigerian businesses targeting local search, this visibility is especially important.

## Why Nigerian SERP tracking is different

Nigerian Google search results differ from UK or US results for the same query. Local pack (Google Maps listings), locally-hosted sites, and Nigerian-specific content get different treatment.

If you're tracking your rankings using a tool calibrated for US results, you may think you're ranking #3 when you're actually #11 in Nigerian search.

SERP scraping — collecting actual search results for specific queries from a Nigerian IP — gives you ground truth.

## What to track

**Your own rankings:** Which of your pages rank for which keywords? Are rankings improving or declining?

**Competitor rankings:** What keywords are your competitors ranking for that you aren't? Where are you beating them?

**Local pack presence:** Are you appearing in Google's local 3-pack for relevant searches? (Critical for service businesses)

**Featured snippets:** Is any competitor capturing the position-0 answer box for your target queries?

## Building a keyword monitoring workflow

**Step 1: Build your keyword list**

Three categories:
- Brand keywords (your company name + variants)
- Target keywords (what you want to rank for)
- Competitor keywords (terms where competitors rank but you don't)

**Step 2: Run baseline SERP scrape**

Get your current position for all tracked keywords. This is your benchmark.

**Step 3: Make SEO changes**

Publish new content, update existing pages, build links, improve technical SEO.

**Step 4: Run follow-up scrape (monthly)**

Compare positions. Rising = working. Falling = problem to investigate.

**Step 5: Investigate drops**

If you lose 5 positions on a key term:
- Has a competitor published something new on that topic?
- Has Google updated its algorithm?
- Has your page changed?

SERP data gives you the "what" (your position changed). Investigation gives you the "why."

## Competitive keyword gap analysis

One of the most valuable SERP applications: finding keywords where competitors rank but you don't.

Process:
1. Scrape top 20 results for 50–100 relevant keywords
2. Filter for results where Competitor X appears but you don't
3. Analyze what content they have that you don't
4. Create better content targeting those keywords

In a market like Nigeria where many businesses have underdeveloped SEO, this gap analysis often reveals significant opportunities.

## The Nigerian search landscape

Key observations from Nigerian SERP data:
- Local businesses often outrank international ones for "Nigeria" queries even with weaker domain authority
- .com.ng domains get some local search preference
- Google Maps presence is highly influential for service businesses
- Nigerian tech blogs (Techcabal, Techpoint) have high domain authority and rank well for startup/tech terms
