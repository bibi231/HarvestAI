## Why Most Nigerian B2B Lead Lists Fail

The typical approach — buying a generic database of "Nigerian businesses" from an online vendor — produces low conversion rates. The contacts are outdated, the industries are mixed, and you end up calling companies that aren't even your buyers. Before you spend a naira on outreach, the list has to be built right.

## Step 1: Define Your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Start with specificity:

- **Industry**: FMCG distribution, logistics, fintech, manufacturing?
- **Size**: How many employees? Annual revenue bracket?
- **Geography**: Lagos Island? Kano? Port Harcourt?
- **Role**: Who signs off on the purchase — CFO, Head of Operations, IT Manager?

A cleaning supplies distributor in Apapa has completely different buying signals than a software firm on Victoria Island. Treat them differently.

## Step 2: Identify the Right Data Sources

Nigerian business data lives across several places:

- **CAC (Corporate Affairs Commission)** records are public and tell you registered company names, directors, and sector codes
- **VConnect and Kompass Nigeria** list businesses with contact info and industry categories
- **LinkedIn** is the most reliable source for individual decision-maker data, particularly in Lagos and Abuja
- **Google Maps** surfaces local businesses with phone numbers, especially for SMBs that may not have a web presence

No single source is complete. Cross-referencing two or more dramatically improves accuracy.

## Step 3: Build the List With Enrichment in Mind

A raw list with only a company name is useless. You need at minimum:

1. Company name and verified trading address
2. Industry and primary product/service
3. Decision-maker name and job title
4. Contact email or phone number
5. Company size indicator (employee count or revenue range)

Enrichment — appending missing fields from secondary sources — is where tools like HarvestAI add real value. You feed in a list of company names and get back fuller records.

## Step 4: Verify Before You Outreach

**Email bounce rates above 5%** damage your sender reputation. Before any campaign:

- Run emails through a verification tool (never send to unverified addresses)
- Validate phone numbers are currently active
- Remove duplicates and defunct companies

## Step 5: Segment and Prioritise

Not every lead is equally ready. Score your list by:

- **Fit**: Does the company match your ICP?
- **Signal**: Have they shown buying intent (job postings, recent funding, new office)?
- **Reachability**: Do you have a verified direct contact?

Top-tier leads get personalised outreach. Lower-tier leads go into a nurture sequence. This approach consistently outperforms blasting the full list with one generic message.

A well-built Nigerian B2B lead list is smaller than you think — and converts far better because of it.
