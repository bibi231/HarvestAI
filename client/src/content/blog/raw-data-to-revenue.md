## The Journey Most Teams Don't Map

Many Nigerian businesses invest in lead generation — scraping directories, buying lists, running LinkedIn campaigns — and still struggle to convert. The failure usually isn't the data. It's the gap between having data and working it systematically through a process.

This is the full journey from raw record to closed revenue.

## Stage 1: Raw Data Collection

You have a list of company names, addresses, and maybe some contact information from a scraping run or directory export. This is raw data. It has not been evaluated, verified, or prioritised.

**Output**: A spreadsheet or CSV with unverified records.

## Stage 2: Enrichment and Verification

Append the missing fields (decision-maker contacts, direct emails, company size, industry codes). Verify emails and phone numbers. Remove duplicates and defunct businesses.

In Nigeria, expect roughly 20–30% of records from a typical directory scrape to have incorrect or outdated contact information. Verification before outreach is not optional.

**Output**: A cleaned, enriched list with verified contact details.

## Stage 3: Qualification and Scoring

Apply your ICP criteria. Score each record on fit, data quality, and intent signals. Segment into tiers: immediate outreach, nurture sequence, discard.

A 2,000-record raw list typically produces 200–400 genuinely qualified prospects worth direct sales attention.

**Output**: Segmented, scored list imported into CRM.

## Stage 4: First Contact

The first touch — email, call, or LinkedIn message — is the bridge between data and conversation. In Nigeria:

- **Cold email** works well for formal sectors (tech, professional services, FMCG). Subject lines that reference a specific business context ("Your expansion into Abuja") outperform generic openers.
- **WhatsApp** is legitimate for SMB outreach and often gets better open rates than email
- **Phone calls** are still effective, particularly for older industries (construction, manufacturing) where email culture is thinner

A response to a first touch means the prospect is alive and aware. It does not mean they're a buyer yet.

**Output**: Response rates typically 3–15% on cold outreach; higher with better targeting.

## Stage 5: Discovery and Qualification Call

The goal here is not to sell — it is to confirm that this prospect has the problem your product solves, has budget (or access to it), and is in a position to make a decision within a reasonable timeframe.

Common discovery failures in Nigerian B2B sales:
- Pitching before understanding the prospect's actual situation
- Failing to confirm decision-making authority
- Quoting pricing before establishing value

**Output**: Qualified opportunity entered in CRM with budget range, timeline, and decision-maker confirmed.

## Stage 6: Proposal and Evaluation

The proposal should reflect what you learned in discovery, not a template. In Nigerian B2B, the evaluation stage often involves multiple stakeholders — the technical team, finance, and a final sign-off from the MD.

Build this into your timeline expectations. Complex deals often have a 4–12 week evaluation window.

**Output**: Proposal submitted, evaluation timeline agreed.

## Stage 7: Negotiation and Close

In Nigeria, price negotiation is expected — build your pricing with reasonable headroom. The close conversation should address:

- Implementation timeline and support
- Payment terms (upfront vs. milestone vs. monthly)
- Any contractual requirements from their side

**Output**: Signed agreement or purchase order.

---

The full journey from raw data to closed deal takes 4–16 weeks depending on deal size. The businesses that close consistently are those that manage each stage with discipline — not those with the longest lead list.
